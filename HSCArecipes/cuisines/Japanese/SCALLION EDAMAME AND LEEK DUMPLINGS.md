# SCALLION, EDAMAME, AND LEEK DUMPLINGS

*(SPRING/SUMMER)*

**Yield:** 32 dumplings

## Ingredients

- 10 ounces shelled edamame
- 1 tablespoon + 1/4 teaspoon sea salt, divided
- 2 tablespoons canola oil
- 10 ounces leeks (3 medium), washed, trimmed, cut into 1/8-inch half moons
- 2 tablespoons minced garlic (2 large cloves)
- 2 tablespoons ginger juice (1 ounce ginger)
- 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon mirin
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice (1 lemon)
- 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro (1/4 ounce)
- 1 tablespoon chopped chives (1/4 ounce)
- 1 tablespoon chopped mint (1/4 ounce)
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tablespoon arrowroot
- 3 tablespoons water
- 32 wonton wrappers
- Canola oil for frying (1/4 - 1/2 cup)
- 1/4 cup tamari
- 1/4 cup rice vinegar
- 2 tablespoons water
- 1 ounce fresh ginger, peeled and cut into julienne (1 ounce)
- 2 teaspoons diagonally, thinly sliced scallions (1 scallion)

## Instructions

1. In 1-gallon pot, bring 6 cups water and 1 tablespoon sea salt to boil. Blanch edamame until tender, about 5-7 minutes. Shock and drain in colander for about 5 minutes. Set aside.
2. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium flame. Add leeks and sweat until tender. Add garlic and remaining 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Continue sweating a few minutes more. Transfer leeks and garlic to food processor and puree with edamame to form a creamy pipe-able consistency.
3. Transfer mixture from food processor to medium-size bowl. Add ginger juice, sesame oil, mirin, lemon juice, cilantro, chives, and mint. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Set aside.
4. In small bowl, slurry arrowroot and water to smooth consistency. Set aside.
5. Place 1 scant tablespoon filling in middle of wonton wrapper. Lightly brush outer edge of wonton wrapper with arrowroot mixture. Fold wrapper in half to form half moon, pinching edges together. Repeat with remaining wonton wrappers and filling.
6. In 12-inch sauté pan, heat 1/4 cup oil over medium-high flame. Add 6-7 wontons at a time and pan fry until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Transfer with slotted spoon to paper towel to drain.
7. Combine and whisk all dipping sauce ingredients in small bowl and serve with dumplings.
