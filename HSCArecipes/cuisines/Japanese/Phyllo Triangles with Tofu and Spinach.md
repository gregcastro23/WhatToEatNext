# Phyllo Triangles with Tofu and Spinach

*(Year-Round)*

**Yield:** 35-40 triangles

## Ingredients

- 14 ounces firm tofu (1 block)
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- ½ pound shallots, minced
- ½ teaspoon sea salt
- ½ teaspoon ground fennel
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 pound spinach, stemmed, steamed, and wrung of excess moisture
- 3-4 tablespoons chickpea miso
- 2 tablespoons umeboshi paste
- ½ pound whole wheat phyllo (8 sheets)
- ¼ to ½ cup extra virgin olive oil

## Instructions

1. Heat convection oven to 350°F. Line half sheet tray with parchment. Set aside.
2. Press tofu between paper towels on sheet pan with weighted plate for amount 30 minutes. Transfer tofu to medium bowl and crumble it with fingertips. Set aside.
3. In 12-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium flame. Sweat shallots, salt, fennel, cumin, and paprika until shallots are translucent. Transfer mixture to bowl with tofu. Add miso and umeboshi. Mix to combine. Chop spinach and combine with tofu mixture. Season to taste.
4. Unwrap phyllo and lay one sheet on parchment paper horizontally. Cover remaining sheets with damp towel. Brush phyllo with oil.
5. Spoon seven 1-tablespoon dollops of mixture across bottom edge of phyllo at equal distances. Cut phyllo into strips between each dollop, and fold up “flag” style (see diagram) to make triangular shape. Transfer finished phyllo triangles to prepared sheet pan. Brush tops with more oil. Repeat with remaining phyllo.
6. Just before baking, brush all triangles with butter again. Bake 20 minutes or until evenly golden.
