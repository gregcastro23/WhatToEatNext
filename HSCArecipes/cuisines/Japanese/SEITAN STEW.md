# SEITAN STEW

**Yield:** 8-10 servings

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- 12 ounces onion (1 large), large dice
- 2 ribs celery, ½-inch thick diagonal slices
- 6 ounces burdock (1 piece), ½-inch roll cut
- 12 ounces carrot (1 large), ½-inch roll cut
- 1 pound daikon (1 medium), ½-inch half moon slices
- ½ pound shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and halved
- 2 pounds butternut squash (1 medium), peeled, 1-inch dice
- 6 cups vegetable stock
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 ½ pounds seitan, ½-inch chunks
- 2 tablespoons tamari
- 4 ounces green beans, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 5 tablespoons kuzu, dissolved in ¼ cup cold stock
- 2 tablespoons juiced ginger (2 ounces)
- Sea salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
- 2 scallions, thin diagonal slice, for garnish

## Instructions

1. In 1-gallon pot, heat olive oil over medium flame and sweat onion until softened. Add celery, burdock, carrots, and daikon. Sweat until vegetables begin to soften, about 5-7 minutes. Add mushrooms and sweat until tender, about 1 minute.
2. Add squash and stock to just cover vegetables, about 6 cups. Cover pot and bring to boil. Lower heat and add bay leaves, seitan, and tamari. Cover and simmer 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all vegetables are tender and squash has started to break down. Add green beans and simmer until beans are just tender.
3. Add kuzu slurry and simmer until mixture slightly thickens. Stir in ginger juice. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Garnish with scallions.
