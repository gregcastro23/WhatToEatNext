# STIR-FRIED ARAME AND GINGER WITH SOBA NOODLES

**Yield:** 4-6 servings

## Ingredients

- 2 ounces soba noodles, broken in half
- 1/2 gallon salted water
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
- 2 ounces ginger, peeled, slivered (2 tablespoons)
- 1 large clove garlic, slivered (1 tablespoon)
- 1/2 cup arame, loosely packed (.05 ounces), soaked 10 minutes in water to cover, drained
- 1/3 red pepper (3 ounces), julienne
- 5 Chinese cabbages leaves, chiffonade
- 1/4 cup water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons tamari
- 2 teaspoons umeboshi vinegar
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup
- 4 scallions (1/2 bunch), trimmed, thinly sliced on the diagonal

## Instructions

1. Boil soba until tender, about 8-10 minutes. Drain, rinse in cold water, and set aside.
2. Coarsely chop arame. Set aside.
3. Heat wok with both sesame oils. Add ginger and stir well. Add garlic. Cook until ginger and garlic are lightly golden, but avoid burning.
4. Stir in arame, peppers, and Chinese cabbage. Once cabbage wilts, add soba.
5. Mix water, tamari, umeboshi vinegar, and maple syrup in small bowl. Pour over vegetables and noodles. Stir until liquid has evaporated.
6. Add scallions and stir to combine.
