# GREEN LENTIL TERRINE

**Yield:** 1 terrine (3 x 11 inches) 8-10 servings

## Ingredients

- 2 cups green lentils, rinsed, drained
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 quart vegetable stock
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 8 ounces onions (1 medium), minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves, minced (1/4 ounce)
- 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1-1 1/2 cups whole wheat bread crumbs
- 2 tablespoons minced parsley (1/4 ounce)
- 2 tablespoons white miso dissolved in 2 tablespoons warm water
- 6 tablespoons tahini
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F. Line terrine mold with parchment.
2. In 1-gallon pot, combine lentils, salt, vegetable stock, and bay leaves. Cover pot and bring to boil. Lower heat, and simmer for 30-40 minutes or until lentils are tender. Drain lentils in colander for 10 minutes.
3. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium-high flame. Sauté onions until browned. Add thyme, coriander, and nutmeg. Sauté 2 minutes more. Set aside.
4. Transfer lentils to food processor and pulse to form thick paste.
5. Transfer lentils to medium-size bowl. Add onions, 1 cup bread crumbs, parsley, miso, and tahini. Mixture should be thick but moist. If mixture is too loose, add more bread crumbs. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
6. Pour mixture into terrine mold and bake until firm, about 20 to 25 minutes. Chill terrine until it reaches room temperature, about 1 hour.
