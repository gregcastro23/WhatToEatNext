# TEMPEH “BOLOGNESE”

**Yield:** 4 Servings

## Ingredients

- 8-ounce package tempeh, grated
- 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 Spanish onion (12 ounces), peeled and small diced
- 1 to 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 tablespoon + 1 1/2 teaspoons minced garlic
- 1 teaspoon dry oregano (or 1 tablespoon fresh)
- 1 teaspoon dry basil (3 tablespoon fresh leaves)
- 1 cup red wine
- 28-ounce can tomato puree (3 1/4 cups)
- Black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons rice syrup (if tomatoes seem too acidic), optional
- 1 pound pasta cooked al dente (to the tooth)

## Instructions

1. Sauté tempeh in 3 tablespoons of olive oil until browned.
2. Add remaining olive oil and onion with 1 teaspoon of salt. Keep cooking until onions are soft.
3. Add garlic, oregano and basil (if using fresh oregano and basil, then add in last 10 minutes of cooking). Cook for few minutes longer. Add 1 cup of red wine and reduce until almost dry.
4. Pour in tomato puree and cook covered for 15-20 minutes.
5. Season with salt, pepper and rice syrup (if needed).
6. Have large pot set up with boiling, salted water and strainer or basket that can be easily lifted out.
7. In 8-inch sauté pan, heat approximately 1/2 cup of Bolognese sauce.
8. Drop one serving pasta (about 4 ounces) in strainer or basket and place in boiling water. Heat through about one minute. Drain pasta.
9. Combine pasta with sauce and toss to coat. Plate pasta and pour over any sauce left in pan. Garnish plate and serve.
