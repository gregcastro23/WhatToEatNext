# Tempeh Scaloppini

**Yield:** 4 servings

## Ingredients

- 8-ounce package tempeh, simmered in marinade (see Basic Tempeh recipe)
- 1/4 - 1/2 cup unbleached all-purpose flour (for dredging)
- 6 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 3/4 cup reserved marinade (from Basic Tempeh recipe)
- 3/4 cup water
- 1/3 - 3/4 cup white wine (or red wine or sherry)
- 2-3 ounces sliced fresh shiitake and/or cremini mushrooms
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh basil, chopped
- 1 teaspoon fresh oregano, chopped
- 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons parsley, chopped for garnish

## Instructions

1. Cut tempeh prepared in Basic Tempeh Recipe in half widthwise on diagonal. Slice each half on diagonal into 6-8 thin slices.
2. Dredge each slice in flour. Shake off excess.
3. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium-high flame and fry tempeh until golden on both sides. Drain any excess oil if necessary.
4. Add reserved liquid from Basic Tempeh recipe, water, and wine to tempeh. Cover and bring to boil.
5. Add sliced mushrooms and garlic, reduce heat to low and cook covered until mushrooms release juices and become tender.
6. Add basil, oregano and thyme; simmer until liquid thickens (needs to be saucy).
7. Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice to taste.
8. Garnish with parsley and serve with rice, noodles or sautéed greens.
