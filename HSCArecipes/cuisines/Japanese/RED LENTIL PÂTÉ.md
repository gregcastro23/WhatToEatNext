# RED LENTIL PÂTÉ

*(YEAR-ROUND)*

**Yield:** approximately 48 1 x 2-inch pâté slices

## Ingredients

- 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
- 1 3/4 cups water OR stock
- 1 onion, small dice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1/4 teaspoon basil
- 1/4 teaspoon oregano
- 1/4 teaspoon thyme
- 1/4 cup whole-wheat bread crumbs
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
- 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon umeboshi vinegar
- 1 1/2 teaspoon white miso dissolved in 1 tablespoon water
- sprigs of dill for garnish

## Instructions

1. Combine lentils in 2 1/2 quart pot with water or stock, bring to boil, and simmer covered 20 minutes.
2. Heat oil in 8-inch sauté pan. Add onions, garlic, and herbs and cook until caramelized.
3. Oil and line loaf pan with parchment and sprinkle with half the breadcrumbs.
4. Preheat oven to 375°F.
5. When lentils are cooked, mash by stirring, add onion mixture, remaining breadcrumbs, and parsley. Season with black pepper, umeboshi vinegar, and white miso.
6. Fold pâté into pan and bake 20-30 minutes. Allow to set, cool, and slice. Garnish with dill.
