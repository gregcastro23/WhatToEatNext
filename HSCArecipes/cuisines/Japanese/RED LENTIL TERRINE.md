# RED LENTIL TERRINE

**Yield:** 1 terrine (3 x 11 inches) 8-10 servings

## Ingredients

- 2 cups red lentils
- 1 quart vegetable stock
- 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 4 ounces onion (1/2 medium), fine dice
- 3 tablespoons minced garlic (3 large cloves)
- 1/4 cup mirin
- 1 cup walnuts, toasted, cooled, ground
- 1 tablespoon umeboshi paste
- 3 tablespoons white miso dissolved in 3 tablespoons warm water
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro (1/4 ounce)
- 2 tablespoons chopped parsley (1/4 ounce)

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F. Line terrine mold with parchment paper and set aside.
2. In 1-gallon pot, combine lentils, stock, salt, and bay leaf. Cover pot and bring to boil. Lower heat and simmer until lentils are tender, about half an hour. Drain lentils in colander for 10 minutes, transfer to medium bowl, and set aside.
3. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium flame and sweat onions and garlic until translucent. Continue to sweat, stirring occasionally, until onions are soft and uniformly browned, about 15 minutes.
4. Deglaze pan with mirin. Combine onions with lentils. Add walnuts, umeboshi paste, miso, salt, pepper, cilantro, and parsley. Mix to combine. Season to taste.
5. Fold mixture into terrine mold. Bake until firm, about 20 to 30 minutes.
6. Refrigerate terrine until it sets, about 1 hour.
