# SHIITAKE BROTH WITH SHRIMP, SOBA, AND BABY BOK CHOY

**Yield:** 8 cups

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon canola oil
- 8 ounces onions (1 medium), large dice
- 4 ounces carrots (1/2 medium), roll cut
- 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 teaspoon Szechwan peppercorns, ground
- 8 cups vegetable stock
- 1 piece star anise
- 2 small dried Arbol chilies
- 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
- 1/2 inch piece of kombu
- 1/3 ounce dried shiitake mushrooms (5)
- 2 tablespoons tamari or to taste
- 1/2 pound baby bok choy, stems and leaves separated, both thinly sliced
- 3 tablespoons ginger juice (5 ounces ginger)
- 6 ounces shrimp, tails removed and cleaned
- 2-3 tablespoons lime juice (2-3 limes)
- Sea salt to taste
- 3 ounces soba noodles, boiled, rinsed in cold water, and drained in a colander

## Instructions

1. In 1-gallon pot, heat oil over medium flame. Sweat onions, carrots, garlic, and peppercorns with salt until onions are translucent.
2. Add stock, star anise, chilies, lemongrass, kombu, dried shiitakes, and tamari. Cover pot and simmer for 25 minutes.
3. Remove and discard star anise, chilies, and lemongrass. Remove kombu and shiitakes from pot. Slice kombu and shiitakes (removing shiitake stems first) into very thin strips and add back to soup pot.
4. Add bok choy stems. Simmer until tender. Add bok choy leaves, shrimp, and ginger juice. Simmer until bok choy leaves wilt and shrimp just turns opaque.
5. Add lime juice and season to taste.
6. Place small portion of noodles in bowl. Pour soup over noodles and serve.
