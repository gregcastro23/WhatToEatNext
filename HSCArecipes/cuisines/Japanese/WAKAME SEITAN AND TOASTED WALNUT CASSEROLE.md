# WAKAME, SEITAN, AND TOASTED WALNUT CASSEROLE

**Yield:** 8 servings

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon untoasted sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
- 10 ounces onion (1 medium), sauté slice
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
- 1/4 pound cremini mushrooms, stemmed, sliced
- 1 cup loosely packed wakame (1 ounce), rinsed, soaked in 1/4 cup water for 10 minutes, drained, coarsely chopped
- 6 ounces seitan, minced
- 2 1/2 cups water
- 2 tablespoons + 1 1/2 teaspoons shoyu
- 1 1/2 cups walnuts, lightly toasted, coarsely chopped
- 5 tablespoons tahini
- 5 tablespoons shoyu
- 1 cup warm water
- 6 ounces whole wheat elbow pasta, cooked al dente
- 1 3/4 cups panko breadcrumbs
- 5 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoons fresh thyme (2 sprigs)
- Freshly ground cracked black pepper taste

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F.
2. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oils over medium flame and sweat onions with salt until translucent. Lower heat and continue to sweat until onions begin to soften and color, stirring often to prevent burning. Continue until onions are caramelized (brown and soft), about 20 minutes. Add mushrooms and cook until tender.
3. Add wakame and seitan. Stir well. Add water and shoyu. Cover pan and bring to boil. Lower flame and remove lid. Simmer until mixture is almost dry, about 15-20 minutes.
4. In small bowl, whisk tahini, shoyu, and water. Set aside.
5. Transfer cooked pasta to large bowl and combine with sauté mixture, walnuts, and tahini sauce. Transfer mixture to lightly oiled Dutch oven.
6. Combine breadcrumbs, olive oil, fresh thyme, and black pepper in small bowl. Top casserole with mixture. Bake 20 minutes.
