# SHEPHERD’S PIE

**Yield:** 9-inch pie or tart

## Ingredients

- 1 unbaked 9-inch pie dough (single crust)
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 onion (12 ounces), cut into medium dice
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 pound carrots (1 large), medium dice
- 1/2 pound parsnips (1 large), medium dice
- 2 teaspoons minced fresh thyme
- 1 1/2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- 1 1/2 teaspoons tamari
- 1/2 teaspoons prepared mustard
- 3-4 tablespoons olive oil for browning seitan
- 1/2 pound seitan, ground in food processor
- 2-3 tablespoons red wine
- 1 cup mushroom stock reduced to 1/2 cup
- 1 teaspoon arrowroot dissolved in 3 teaspoons cold water
- 2 pounds peeled Yukon gold potatoes, large dice
- 2-4 tablespoons soy milk
- 1 tablespoon butter OR olive oil
- sea salt
- pepper
- 3 tablespoons chopped parsley for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Partially 'blind bake' pie shell (about 10 minutes) and set aside.
2. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat olive oil. Add onion, garlic, carrots, parsnips, and fresh thyme. Sauté until slightly tender. Add couple tablespoons water to deglaze pan.
3. Mix together sesame oil, tamari, and mustard; stir into vegetable mixture in sauté pan. Continue to cook another 10 minutes, and transfer mixture to bowl.
4. In separate 10-inch sauté pan, heat olive oil, add seitan and cook until seitan is slightly browned. Add wine and continue cooking until wine is absorbed. Add seitan to bowl with cooked vegetables.
5. Thicken reduced stock by adding arrowroot slurry and simmering until mixture thickens. Add this mixture to bowl containing vegetables and seitan. Season mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Set aside to cool.
6. In 1-gallon pot, boil potatoes until tender. Drain. Process potatoes through ricer or food mill. Season with soy milk, butter or olive oil, salt and pepper. Cover and set aside.
7. Fill partially baked pie shell with vegetable/seitan mixture. Using pastry bag fitted with tip, pipe potato mixture onto top of pie.
8. Bake for about 20-25 minutes or until vegetables are fork tender and crust is fully baked. Remove from oven and garnish with chopped parsley.
