# TEMPEH VEGETABLE KEBOBS

**Yield:** 12 (8-inch) kebobs

## Ingredients

- 3/4 cup shoyu
- 2 cups apple juice
- 1 1/2 cups orange juice (3-4 oranges)
- 8 cloves garlic, peeled and crushed
- 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 2-8 ounce packages tempeh
- 12-8 inch skewers
- 6 large cremini mushrooms, stemmed and cut in half
- 1/2 zucchini, cut in half lengthwise, then into 1/2 inch slices
- 1/3 yellow pepper, cut into twelve equal pieces
- 12 cherry tomatoes
- 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice (1/2 lemon)
- 1/3 cup orange juice (1 orange)
- 2 tablespoons shoyu
- 1 tablespoon arrowroot, dissolved in 2 tablespoons apple juice

## Instructions

1. Whisk together tempeh marinade ingredients in mixing bowl; pour into 2 1/2 quart pot.
2. Cut tempeh in two. Add tempeh to marinade. Bring marinade to boil, lower heat and simmer covered for 45 minutes. Drain tempeh and pat dry, reserving marinade (there should be 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons).
3. Whisk together vegetable marinade ingredients in mixing bowl. Add all vegetables to marinade and let sit for 20-30 minutes, stirring often. Remove vegetables with slotted spoon (set aside marinade for later use).
4. When tempeh reaches room temperature, cut into 24 cubes and brush all sides with oil.
5. Preheat grooved griddle over medium-high heat while preparing kebobs; use one piece of each vegetable and two pieces of tempeh for each skewer (alternating vegetables and tempeh).
6. Grill kebobs few minutes on each side; continue cooking few minutes longer, brushing kebobs with vegetable marinade on all sides, until vegetables are tender.
7. Bring reserved tempeh marinade to boil in 8-inch saucepan; whisk in dissolved arrowroot, stirring constantly until sauce comes back to boil and becomes clear and thick. Use sauce for kebobs and grain accompaniment. Kebobs can be baked in 400°F oven for 20-30 minutes as an alternative to grilling. Brush them occasionally with vegetable marinade as they cook.
