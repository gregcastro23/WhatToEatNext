# Family Style Bean Curd

**Yield:** 4 servings

## Ingredients

- 14-ounce block of firm tofu, pressed 30 minutes
- 6 cups coconut oil
- 2 teaspoons shoyu OR tamari
- 2 teaspoons ginger juice (1/2-inch knob ginger)
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- 2 scallions, cut into thin diagonal
- 1 1/2 teaspoons peeled ginger root, cut into thin matchsticks (1/2-inch knob)
- 1 carrot, cut into thin matchsticks
- 3 dried shiitake mushrooms (rehydrated in hot water until completely tender), cut into paper thin slices
- 1/3 cup canned water chestnuts, peeled and sliced
- 1 1/3 cups hot stock OR hot water
- 2-3 tablespoons shoyu OR tamari
- 1 tablespoon mirin
- 1 teaspoon brown rice syrup
- 1 tablespoon + 1 1/2 teaspoons kuzu
- 1 tablespoon + 1 1/2 teaspoons cold water
- Garnish: 1 scallion, cut into thin diagonal

## Instructions

1. Cut tofu into 6 (1/2-inch) slices, then into triangles.
2. Heat wok and add coconut oil. Prepare plate with paper towel for draining. Deep fry tofu until golden brown.
3. Transfer fried tofu to paper towel to drain. Let oil cool completely before removing from wok.
4. Place deep fried tofu in small pot with water to cover. Add shoyu and ginger juice.
5. Simmer 15 minutes.
6. Heat wok and add sesame oil. Add scallions and ginger root. When their aroma is imparted, add carrots, mushrooms, water chestnuts, and fried tofu to wok.
7. Add hot stock, shoyu, mirin, and brown rice syrup and cook for an additional 3 minutes.
8. Dissolve kuzu in cold water and add to wok. Simmer 1 minute. Adjust ginger juice and shoyu or tamari if necessary.
9. Serve over rice or noodles. Garnish with scallions.
