# Green Bean and Walnut Pâté

**Yield:** 1 1/2 cups

## Ingredients

- 3/4 teaspoon sea salt, divided
- 10 ounces green beans, trimmed
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 4 ounces onion (1/2 medium), sauté slice
- 1/4 cup Madeira wine
- 1 1/4 cups walnuts, well toasted and cooled
- 1 tablespoon white miso
- 1/3 teaspoon sherry vinegar
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Prepare an ice bath. In 1-gallon pot, bring 3 quarts of water to boil with 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Add green beans and blanch until tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Shock beans in ice water and drain in colander. Slice 8 beans thinly on diagonal for garnish. Cut remaining beans into 1-inch pieces. Set aside.
2. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium flame. Add onions and remaining 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Cover and sweat onions over low heat until very soft, about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Uncover pan and continue cooking and stirring often until onions are caramelized, about 5 minutes.
3. Add wine to onions and continue cooking until wine is reduced and mixture is almost dry.
4. Puree 1 cup of walnuts with green beans, onions, miso, and vinegar in food processor until smooth. Season to taste. Chop remaining 1/4 cup of walnuts for garnish.
5. Present pâté garnished with string beans and chopped walnuts.
