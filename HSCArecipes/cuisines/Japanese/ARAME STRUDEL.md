# ARAME STRUDEL

**Yield:** 4 Strudels

## Ingredients

- 4 ounces arame, soaked 15 minutes and drained
- 1/2 cup toasted sesame oil, divided
- 1 quart water, approximately
- 3 tablespoons shoyu
- 1/3 cup brown rice syrup
- 2 to 3 tablespoons mirin
- 1 tablespoon ginger juice
- 1 medium size onion, sliced into salad slice
- 1 large carrot cut into matchsticks
- Sea salt, to taste
- 1 pound phyllo

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375°F. In large sauté pan, sauté arame in 2-3 tablespoons sesame oil for 3 to 5 minutes. Add water to pan (arame should be three-quarters covered), and bring to boil. Reduce heat to simmer and cook until liquid is evaporated. Stir in shoyu, brown rice syrup, and mirin. Reduce until arame is dry. Add ginger juice. Set aside.
2. In another medium pan, sauté carrots, onion, and sea salt in 1 tablespoon sesame oil until carrots and onions are tender, approximately 10-12 minutes. Set aside.
3. On a sheet of parchment paper, stack 3 sheets of phyllo, brushing each individual layer lightly with remaining sesame oil. Spoon 1-inch wide strip of arame mixture and carrot mixture horizontally onto near end of phyllo. Using the parchment paper, firmly roll strudel forward into a log. Score rolled log diagonally at 2-inch intervals across its length. Repeat this process using remaining phyllo, arame, and carrot/onion mixture until all mixture and phyllo is used up.
4. Transfer strudels to sheet pan; brush their tops with sesame oil, and bake approximately 20 minutes, until deeply golden. When cool, slice strudel into individual servings.
