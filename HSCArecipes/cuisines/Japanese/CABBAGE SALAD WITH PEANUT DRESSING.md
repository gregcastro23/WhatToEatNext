# CABBAGE SALAD WITH PEANUT DRESSING

*(YEAR-ROUND)*

**Yield:** 6-8 servings

## Ingredients

- 14 ounces firm tofu, medium dice
- 1/2 cup coconut milk
- 1/3 cup tamari
- 2 tablespoon lime juice (2 limes)
- 2 tablespoons rice syrup
- 2 pounds cabbage, cored, chiffonade
- 1 tablespoon sea salt
- 8 cups boiling water
- 1/2 pound Thai rice noodles
- 6 ounces snow peas, julienned and blanched
- 2 ounces English cucumber (3/4), thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup peanuts, toasted, chopped
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 4 ounces onion (1/2 onion), minced
- 2 tablespoons minced garlic (2 large cloves)
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon lime zest (1/2 lime)
- 2 tablespoons lime juice (2 limes)
- 2 tablespoons coconut sugar
- 1 teaspoon tamari or to taste
- 6 ounces coconut milk (3/4 cup)
- 1/4 cup peanut butter

## Instructions

1. Combine tofu in 12-inch sauté pan with coconut milk, 1/4 cup of the tamari, lime juice, and rice syrup. Simmer uncovered for 20-25 minutes or until mixture thickens. Set aside.
2. Prepare an ice bath. Bring 3 quarts water to boil. Add 1 tablespoon salt. Blanch cabbage in for 2 minutes. Transfer cabbage with slotted spoon to ice bath. Drain cabbage in colander for about 5 minutes. Squeeze out any excess moisture from cabbage and set aside.
3. Bring water back to boil. Transfer noodles to large bowl. When water comes to boil, pour water over noodles and let noodles steep for 10-15 minutes or until tender. Drain and rinse under cold water. Toss noodles with remaining 1/4 cup of tamari.
4. In 2 1/2 quart saucepan, heat oil on medium flame. Sweat onion, garlic, salt, and chili powder until onions are translucent. Add lime zest, lime juice, coconut sugar, and tamari.
5. In a small bowl, whisk coconut milk and peanut butter, stirring until smooth. Add to onion-garlic mixture and simmer gently 2-3 minutes or until sauce thickens to desired consistency.
6. Remove sauce from heat and cool to room temperature. Toss half the peanut sauce with noodles.
7. Pool remaining peanut sauce on bottom of plate. Arrange plate with tofu, noodles, cabbage, snow peas, cucumbers, and peanuts.
