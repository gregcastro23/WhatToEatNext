# WHOLE STEAMED FISH

**Yield:** 1 whole fish

## Ingredients

- 2 1/2-3 pound whole dressed fish, head and tail intact, gills removed
- 1 1/2 teaspoons sea salt
- Pepper to taste
- 1 ounce cilantro, leaves picked and washed
- 6 scallions, washed, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 4 cloves garlic, sliced thin
- 1 ounce ginger, minced
- 1 1/2 cups water
- 1/4 cup tamari
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil

## Instructions

1. Rinse fish, removing any remaining scales or blood. Score fish by making two, 3-inch long/1/4-inch deep cuts into each side of fish.
2. Season outside and cavity of fish with salt and pepper.
3. In bowl, toss cilantro, scallion, garlic, and ginger together. Stuff half of mixture into cavity of fish.
4. Combine remaining herb/spice mixture with water, tamari, and sesame oil. Transfer mixture to deep hotel pan. Place lightly oiled perforated pan over mixture. Bring mixture to boil over high flame and reduce heat to simmer. Transfer fish to perforated pan. Cover pan with lid or aluminum foil. Steam fish for 15-20 minutes.
5. When fish is fully cooked and tender, gently transfer to platter. Ladle steaming liquid, herbs, and toasted sesame oil over fish.
