# Red Lentil and Toasted Sunflower Burger

**Yield:** Eight 1/2-cup portions

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup short grain brown rice, rinsed and drained
- 1 3/4 cups water
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
- 1/2 onion (5 ounces), 1/4-inch dice
- 4 cloves garlic, minced (1 packed tablespoon)
- 3/4 cup sunflower seeds toasted and ground in food processor
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin seed
- 3/4 teaspoons salt
- 2 broccoli stalks (12 ounces), peeled, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1/2 carrot (3 ounces), 3/4-inch dice
- 1 stalk celery (1 ounce), trimmed, 1-inch dice
- 1 cup red lentils, sorted and rinsed
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 cup fresh chopped parsley leaves (3 ounces)
- 1/4 cup kuzu crushed into powder
- Extra virgin olive oil for brushing
- Fresh Ketchup (see following recipe)

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. In pressure cooker, dry roast rice until it has nutty aroma. Add water and pinch of salt. Bring rice to pressure for 30 minutes. Bring pressure down, and drain rice in colander.
3. While rice cooks, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in 10-inch sauté pan on medium-low heat. Add onions and garlic; sweat for 2-3 minutes. Add sunflower seeds, chili powder, cumin, and salt. Add broccoli stalks, carrots, and celery and sauté for 3 minutes more.
4. Combine vegetables with rice in food processor. Pulse until mixture becomes slightly sticky and binding. Set rice-vegetable mixture aside in bowl.
5. Combine lentils in 2-quart pot with 4 cups water and pinch of salt. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, and cook for 15 minutes or until lentils are soft. Drain lentils in colander for 5 minutes and transfer to bowl with rice-vegetable mixture.
6. When mixture is cool, add parsley and kuzu. Mix all ingredients well with gloved hands. Form patties using 1/2 cup measure.
7. Lightly spray or brush 10-inch non-stick pan with olive oil. Brown patties on each side over medium heat and return to parchment-lined sheet pan. Heat patties in oven for 15 more minutes.
8. Serve with Fresh Ketchup (recipe following).
