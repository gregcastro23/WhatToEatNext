# Vegetable-Polenta Napoleons

**Yield:** 4 servings

## Ingredients

- 2/3 cup cannellini beans, soaked and drained
- 5 cups water
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 1 head roasted garlic, paste extracted
- 1 1/2 teaspoons extra virgin olive oil
- Salt
- 3 cups water
- 3/4 cup polenta
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon extra virgin olive oil
- 8 sun-dried tomatoes, soaked until soft and coarsely chopped
- 1/3 cup basil leaves, minced (1 ounce)
- 4 Portobello mushrooms, stemmed and cleaned
- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 1/2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
- Pinch salt
- 1 1/2 pounds spinach, triple-washed
- Salt to taste
- 1 recipe Tomato “Cream” Sauce (see following recipe)
- 1/4 cup basil, chiffonade (for garnish)

## Instructions

1. In small saucepan, simmer beans in water with rosemary until beans are soft. Drain beans, discarding rosemary. In small bowl, combine beans, roasted garlic paste, oil and salt to taste. Using fork, mash bean mixture to chunky-smooth consistency and set aside.
2. In small saucepan, combine water, polenta, and salt, stirring until smooth. Simmer for 15 minutes. Stir oil, sun-dried tomatoes and basil into polenta and spread polenta onto lightly oiled sheet tray to 1/2-inch thickness. Chill polenta until firmly set. Using 3 1/2-inch ring mold, cut out 4 circles.
3. In small bowl, whisk together oil and vinegar. Using pastry brush, brush tops of mushrooms with oil-vinegar mixture and sprinkle with salt.
4. Coat non-stick pan with thin film of oil. Sear mushrooms covered (top side down), over low heat for 5-6 minutes. Flip mushrooms, brush “gill” side with remaining oil/vinegar, sprinkle with salt, and cook until tender, about 5 minutes. Cool mushrooms and cut out 4 circles using 3 1/2-inch ring mold.
5. Steam spinach in covered pot until wilted, 1 to 2 minutes. Drain and wring well, chop coarsely and season to taste with salt.
6. Stack Napoleon elements in layers on sizzle platter and reheat in oven. Serve atop Tomato Cream Sauce garnished with basil.
