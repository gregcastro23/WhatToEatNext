# Stuffed Squid, Sicilian Style

**Yield:** 10 squid, approx. 2 cups filling

## Ingredients

- 10 medium squid, cleaned (about 1 3/4 pounds), save tentacles
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1/3 red pepper, brunoise
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 pound shrimp, shelled, 1/4-inch dice
- Splash of white wine for deglazing
- 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
- 3 tablespoons chopped mint (1/2 ounce)
- 1 tablespoon diced Kalamata olives
- 1 tablespoon currants
- 1 tablespoon drained capers, minced
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
- sea salt and black pepper to taste
- 1 cup dry white wine
- 2 cups Shrimp Stock
- 1 cup tomato puree
- 2 bay leaves
- pinch of sea salt
- 1/2 ounce mint sprigs for garnish
- lemon slices from 2 lemons for garnish

## Instructions

1. Remove tentacles from squid, chop finely and set aside.
2. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium-high flame. When oil is hot, add peppers. Sauté until just tender, lower heat slightly, and add garlic, shrimp, and squid tentacles. Cook until shrimp is opaque. Deglaze pan with wine.
3. Place shrimp mixture into medium bowl. Let cool.
4. Add breadcrumbs, mint, olives, currants, capers and lemon juice to bowl with shrimp mixture.
5. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
6. Stuff squid bodies with mixture. Make sure not to overstuff as mixture expands during cooking. Use about 3 tablespoons per squid body. Secure squid body opening with one or two toothpicks. Place in pan large enough to accommodate squid in one even layer.
7. Add wine, stock, tomato puree, bay leaves and salt. Cover pan and turn heat to low.
8. Simmer until squid is tender, about 40-50 minutes. At 20-minute mark, turn squid over.
9. Remove from pan. Garnish with mint and lemon slices.
