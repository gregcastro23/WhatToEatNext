# BERRIES AND CREAM TERRINE

**Yield:** 6 cups, enough to make three 6 x 4-inch rectangular terrines

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups cashews, soaked and drained (approximately 2 cups soaked)
- 3/4 cup coconut milk
- 3/4 cup agave syrup
- 3/4 cup refined coconut oil, melted
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 vanilla bean, scraped
- pinch of sea salt
- 1 10-ounce bag frozen blueberries (approximately 2 1/2 cups)
- 3/4 cup coconut milk
- 3/4 cup agave syrup
- 1 1/2 cups cashews, soaked and drained (approximately 2 cups soaked)
- 3/4 cup refined coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- pinch sea salt
- 1 cup blackberries, halved
- 1/4 cup mint leaves

## Instructions

1. For vanilla layer: In a high-speed blender, combine soaked cashews, coconut milk, agave syrup, coconut oil, vanilla extract, lemon juice, scraped vanilla bean and sea salt.
2. Blend on high for a few minutes until mixture is completely smooth.
3. Transfer filling into rectangular 6 x 4-inch silicone molds, filling them halfway up (approximately 1 cup per mold) and place in the freezer.
4. For blueberry layer: Add frozen blueberries, coconut milk and agave syrup into a small saucepan.
5. Bring mixture to a simmer over high heat. Reduce for 10 to 15 minutes, stirring frequently, until mixture is reduced to 1 1/2 cups. Cool over ice bath.
6. Pour cooled mixture into high-speed blender. Add soaked cashews, coconut oil, lemon juice, and salt.
7. Blend on high for a few minutes until the mixture is completely smooth.
8. Gently spoon mixture on top of vanilla layer already setting in silicone molds.
9. Freeze for one hour, or until firm.
10. To serve: Let terrines sit for 5 to 10 minutes at room temperature before serving.
11. Place terrines on a serving plate. Top with cut blackberries and mint.
12. Store any leftover terrines in the freezer.
