# CHOCOLATE SOUFFLÉ

**Yield:** 6-8 servings

## Ingredients

- 5 tablespoons unsalted butter (1 tablespoon softened, remaining 4 tablespoons cut into 1/2-inch chunks)
- 1 tablespoon sifted cocoa powder
- 1/2 pound semi-sweet chocolate, coarsely chopped
- 1/8 teaspoon sea salt
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 6 large egg yolks
- 1/3 cup maple crystals
- 8 large egg whites
- 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 375°F. Butter inside of soufflé dish (or dishes) with the one tablespoon of softened butter.
2. Dust inside of each dish with cocoa powder.
3. Refrigerate dishes until ready to fill.
4. Melt chocolate and remaining 4 tablespoons butter in medium bowl set over pot of simmering water. Turn off heat, stir in salt and vanilla and set aside.
5. In separate medium bowl, beat egg yolks and maple crystals with whisk or hand mixer until thick and yellow (about 3 minutes). Pour into chocolate mixture.
6. In separate medium bowl or electric stand mixer, combine egg whites and cream of tartar. By hand or mixer, whip egg whites until stiff, moist peaks are formed.
7. Vigorously stir one-quarter of whites into chocolate-egg yolk mixture.
8. Gently fold in remaining egg whites in 4 equal increments.
9. Transfer to ramekins and bake as follows: 1/2 cup dishes: 12 to 14 minutes, 2 cup dishes: 20 to 25 minutes, 1 quart dishes: 30 - 35 minutes, 2 quart dishes: 50 to 55 minutes.
10. Serve immediately.
