# CRÊPES SUZETTE

**Yield:** about 15

## Ingredients

- 1/4 cup + 2 tablespoons butter
- 3/4 cup fresh orange juice (strained)
- 1/2 cup maple crystals
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons orange zest
- 1/4 cup Grand Marnier
- 1/4 cup brandy
- 15 Plain Crêpes (recipe follows)
- 1 cup Grand Marnier (reserved for flambé)
- 1 pint heavy cream, whipped, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions

1. In 12-inch sauté pan, over medium heat, bring butter, orange juice, maple crystals, lemon juice, and zest to boil. Cook until thickened, about 3-4 minutes and stir in Grand Marnier and brandy. Bring again to boil for 30 seconds. Transfer syrup to bowl and set aside.
2. In separate 10-inch sauté pan, over medium-low heat, pour in small ladle full of syrup and heat to simmer. Place one crêpe (browned side down) in pan. Coat crêpe in warmed syrup then flip crêpe over with tongs to coat other side. Fold crêpe in quarters (fold in half then half again) to form triangle.
3. Pour 1 tablespoon of Grand Marnier per crêpe over top of folded crepe. Ignite by tilting pan toward burner. Allow alcohol to burn off before removing to plate and serving. Repeat to order.
