# Classic Béchamel

**Yield:** 1 quart

## Ingredients

- 6 ounces (1/2 large) onion, piqué
- 4-5 cups milk, divided
- 1 ounce all-purpose flour (6 tablespoons)
- 1 1/2 ounces clarified butter
- Sea salt and ground white pepper to taste
- Pinch ground nutmeg

## Instructions

1. Add onion piqué to milk in 2 1/2 quart saucepan and simmer on low heat for 10 minutes, stirring often to avoid scorching.
2. Remove piqué onion from pot. Set milk aside to cool.
3. In 10-inch sauté pan, combine flour and butter. Stir with wooden spoon for 2-3 minutes. Let roux cool.
4. Slowly and gradually whisk 4 cups cooled milk into warm roux.
5. Simmer sauce for approximately 20-25 minutes until thickened and reduced to nappé consistency.
6. Strain sauce through fine chinois. Add additional 1 cup milk on low flame, if needed.
