# SMOKY GREEN SPLIT PEA SOUP

**Yield:** 9 cups

## Ingredients

- 2 cups water
- 1 tablespoon loose Lapsang Souchong tea
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 12 ounces onion, medium dice
- 8 ounces carrot, medium dice
- 8 ounces parsnip, medium dice
- 3 ribs celery, medium dice
- 3 tablespoons minced garlic (3 large cloves)
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 cup green split peas, soaked overnight, drained, and rinsed
- 8 cups stock
- 6 ounces collard greens (6 leaves), stemmed, and cut into bite-sized pieces
- 4 ounces cauliflower (1/4 small head), cut into bite-sized florets
- 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
- Sea salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions

1. Combine water and tea in 2-quart pot. Bring to boil, cover, and remove from heat. Steep 5 minutes, strain leaves from tea, and set aside.
2. Heat oil in separate 1-gallon pot over medium-low flame, and add onion, carrots, parsnip, celery, garlic, and salt. Sweat vegetables, covered, stirring occasionally until they begin to soften, about 7 minutes.
3. Add bay leaf, split peas, and stock. Bring to boil over high heat. Reduce heat and simmer with pot partially covered until split peas are tender, about 45-60 minutes.
4. Remove bay leaf. Add collard leaves and cauliflower. Continue to simmer until greens and cauliflower are tender.
5. Remove 2 cups of soup and purée in blender until creamy. Add blended ingredients back to pot.
6. Add vinegar and tea, bring to simmer again, remove from heat, and season to taste with salt and pepper.
