# CURRIED CHICKPEA CRÊPE FILLING

**Yield:** 4 1/2 cups, nine 1/2 cup servings

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup chickpeas, soaked overnight
- 1 cup water
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 tablespoons coconut oil
- 1 medium onion (about 1/2 pound), small dice
- 1 tablespoon curry powder
- 2 medium carrots (about 12 ounces), cut into 1/4-inch wide quarter moon slices
- 1 medium potato (about 1/2 pound), peeled and cut into small dice
- 2 large garlic cloves, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
- 1/2 cup vegetable stock OR bean liquid from cooked chickpeas
- 1 3/4 cups coconut milk (1-14 ounce can)
- 1 cup cooked chickpeas
- 2 bunches spinach (about 1 1/2 pounds), stemmed, washed, and chopped

## Instructions

1. In pressure cooker, combine chickpeas, water, cumin, salt, and bay leaves.
2. Bring pot to pressure, lower heat, and cook 30 minutes. Drain beans, set aside, and reserve bean liquid if using in place of stock.
3. In 12-inch sauté pan, heat oil. Add onions. Lower heat and cook until onions begin to soften and become translucent. Add curry. Cook 2-3 minutes more.
4. Add carrots and potatoes, stirring often to prevent sticking. Cook 5-6 minutes. Add garlic and salt. Cook 2-3 minutes more. Add stock or bean liquid reserved from chickpeas.
5. Add coconut milk and chickpeas. Simmer 3-4 minutes more. Add spinach. Cook until spinach becomes tender (about 1-2 minutes). Adjust seasonings. If mixture looks too thick, add bit more stock or bean liquid.
