# Olive Bread

**Yield:** 2 loaves

## Ingredients

- 2 cups warm water (110°)
- 1 teaspoon warm maple syrup
- 1 tablespoon + 1 1/2 teaspoons dry yeast
- 1/3 cup olive oil
- 1/2 cup pitted and chopped olives
- 1 tablespoon sea salt
- 2 cups unbleached high-gluten bread flour
- 4-5 cups whole wheat pastry flour

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 475°F. In small bowl, combine water, maple syrup, and yeast. Allow mixture to sit about 10 minutes until is foamy.
2. Add oil, olives, salt, bread flour, and just enough pastry flour to create dough that pulls away from sides of bowl.
3. Knead dough on table about 10-15 minutes, using remaining pastry flour, until smooth dough has been created.
4. Place dough in large, clean, lightly oiled bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Proof in a warm place for 45-60 minutes or until dough has doubled.
5. While dough is proofing, prepare bread pans or sheet pans with parchment.
6. Punch down dough. If time permits, place dough back in bowl, rise, and punch down 1 to 2 times more. Divide dough into two equal portions. Form dough into loaves and place in prepared loaf pans or on sheet trays for final rise.
7. Loosely cover dough with damp towel and set aside in warm place. Allow to rise for about 40 minutes or until dough has doubled in size.
8. Bake bread approximately 30 minutes or until dough is golden and firm to touch. Internal dough temperature should register approximately 190° - 205°.
