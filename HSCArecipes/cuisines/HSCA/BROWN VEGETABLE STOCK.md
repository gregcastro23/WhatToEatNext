# BROWN VEGETABLE STOCK

**Yield:** approximately 1 gallon + 1 quart

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup white beans, soaked, drained, and rinsed
- 3/4 cup lentils, soaked, drained and rinsed
- 1 gallon + 2 quarts water
- 4 carrots (1 pound), 1/4-inch diagonal
- 2 onions (2 pounds), peeled and large dice
- 2 shallots (4 ounces), peeled and sauté sliced
- 3 stalks celery, 1/4-inch sliced
- 1 parsnip (6 ounces), 1/4-inch slices
- 3 - 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 4 plum tomatoes (1 pound), seeded and chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, peeled
- 1/4 cup red wine
- 1 cup walnuts, roasted
- Sachet d'épices: 3 parsley stems, 2 bay leaves, 5 peppercorns, 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 1 tablespoon shoyu

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400-425°F.
2. In large stockpot, combine white beans, lentils, and water. Bring to boil, covered, skimming foam as needed. Reduce heat to simmer, and continue to cook partially covered.
3. Mix carrots, onions, shallots, celery, and parsnips with oil. Roast on large oiled baking sheet, stirring occasionally, until browned and aromatic. Add tomatoes and garlic to vegetables in oven near end of roasting time. Add roasted vegetables to bean stockpot.
4. Deglaze baking sheet with red wine. Add deglaze liquid, walnuts, sachet d'épices, and shoyu to bean stockpot. Simmer, partially covered for 60-90 minutes. Strain immediately.
