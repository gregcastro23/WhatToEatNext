# Deviled Eggs with Beet Chips

*(Year-Round)*

**Yield:** 12 eggs

## Ingredients

- 3 cups water
- 1 quart fresh beet juice (2 pound beets)
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 2 teaspoons sea salt
- 1 dozen large eggs
- ½ cup Greek full-fat yogurt
- 2 ½ teaspoons Dijon mustard
- ¾ teaspoon sea salt
- Pinch freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons chopped chives (½ ounce)
- Beet Chips for garnish (recipe below)

## Instructions

1. Prepare ice bath.
2. In 2 ½ quart saucepan, bring water and beet juice to boil. Add vinegar and salt. Set aside.
3. In 1-gallon pot, combine eggs with enough water to cover by one inch. Cover pot and bring water to boil. Immediately remove pot from the heat and allow eggs to sit covered for 8-9 minutes.
4. Transfer eggs to ice bath, allowing them to cool for 1 full minute. Remove eggs ice bath, crack shells on hard surface, and peel eggs carefully.
5. Transfer eggs to pot with beet juice for about 1 hour. Remove eggs from pot and gently dry them.
6. Slice eggs in half and remove yolks. Transfer yolks to food processor with yogurt, mustard, salt, and pepper. Blend to creamy, pipe-able consistency. Season to taste.
7. Place seasoned yolk mixture in pastry bag with star tip and pipe about 1 tablespoon into each dyed egg white half. Garnish with chives and beet chips (recipe below).
