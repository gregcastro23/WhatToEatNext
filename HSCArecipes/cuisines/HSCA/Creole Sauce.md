# Creole Sauce

**Yield:** 5 cups

## Ingredients

- 2 (28 ounce) cans whole tomatoes (including juice)
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 4 ounces scallions (1/2 bunch), trimmed and minced
- 3 ribs celery, minced
- 5 ounces green pepper (1 small), seeded and minced
- 1 tablespoon agave syrup
- 1/2 - 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (to taste)
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Strain whole tomatoes from juice. Reserve juice and set aside. Blend whole tomatoes in food processor.
2. In 12-inch sauté pan, heat oil over low heat. Add garlic and salt. Sweat until garlic softens (do not burn garlic). Add scallions, celery, red pepper flakes, and pepper. Sweat until vegetables are tender.
3. Raise heat to medium-high and add blended tomatoes to pan. Sauté until tomatoes begin to caramelize.
4. Lower heat and add agave and reserved tomato juice. Simmer until sauce thickens, about 10 minutes.
5. Adjust seasoning to taste.
