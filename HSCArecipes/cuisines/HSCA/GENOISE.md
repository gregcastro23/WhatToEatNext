# Genoise

**Yield:** Two 8-inch cakes

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs, room temperature
- 1 cup maple crystals
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup whole wheat pastry flour, sifted

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F (300°F in convection oven). Oil and flour two 8-inch cake pans and line bottoms with lightly oiled parchment.
2. If eggs are cold from refrigeration, combine them in medium bowl and set bowl atop pot of simmering water. Whisk eggs until they are room temperature (cold eggs do not achieve sufficient peak when beaten). Eggs should neither be cool nor warm to touch.
3. Add eggs, maple crystals, and vanilla to stand mixer bowl and beat at high speed for about 6-10 minutes until mixture has tripled in volume (foam should almost fill up bowl of mixer) and has become light in color and foamy. Mixture should be almost consistency of stiff peaks.
4. Gently transfer egg mixture into clean large bowl. Sift flour in small batches (3 times) into egg mixture while folding batter with large rubber spatula.
5. Divide batter between 2 prepared pans.
6. Bake for 15 minutes or until cake is uniformly golden brown and springy to touch.
7. Using paring knife, cut cake away from sides of pan immediately, leaving cake in pans to cool for 10 minutes.
8. Remove cakes from pans, remove parchment from bottoms of cakes, and cool completely on wire rack. Use each whole cake for two-layer cake or torte each cake to create four layers.
