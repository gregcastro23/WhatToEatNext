# PORCINI MUSHROOM STOCK

**Yield:** 3 cups

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/2 pound button mushrooms, washed, coarsely chopped
- 1 pound onion, cut into 1/2 inch medium dice
- 6 cups water
- 2 cloves of garlic, peeled
- 1/2 ounce dried porcini mushrooms, soaked 20 minutes in water (reserve soaking water)
- 1 celery rib, coarsely chopped
- 1/2 pound carrot, coarsely chopped
- 2 bay leaves
- 4 black peppercorns
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 thyme sprig

## Instructions

1. Heat oil in 1-gallon stockpot. Add fresh mushrooms and onion to cook over medium heat stirring occasionally until well browned, about 15 minutes.
2. Add water, garlic, porcini mushrooms, reserved soaking water, celery, carrots, bay leaves, peppercorns, parsley stems, and thyme and bring to boil, covered.
3. Lower heat and simmer, covered, for 30 minutes.
4. Remove dried mushrooms and discard or save for another use.
5. Strain stock through double layer of cheesecloth. Press vegetables firmly to get out as much liquid as possible.
6. Measure volume of stock. Return to stove and reduce to 3 cups.
