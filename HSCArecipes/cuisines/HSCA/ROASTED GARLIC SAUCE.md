# ROASTED GARLIC SAUCE

**Yield:** 2 cups

## Ingredients

- 2 heads garlic, feathery outer layers removed
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
- 1/4 pound onions (1/2 small), small dice
- 1/4 pound carrots, (1 small) small dice
- 3 ribs celery, small dice
- 2 tablespoons red wine
- 2 tablespoons brown rice vinegar
- 4 cups brown stock, warmed
- Sachet d'Epices (3 bay leaves, 6 parsley stems, 5 sprigs thyme, 6 black peppercorns tied in cheesecloth)
- 2 ounces mushrooms, small dice
- 2 ounces blond roux (1 ounce flour + 1 ounce oil), cooled
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions

1. Roast garlic (wrapped in foil) in 400°F oven for approximately 35 minutes. When cool, squeeze out garlic paste from skin. Blend with small amount of stock until consistency is creamy.
2. In 1-gallon pot, over medium-high flame, heat remaining tablespoon oil. Sauté onions, carrots, and celery until lightly caramelized.
3. Deglaze over high heat with red wine and brown rice vinegar. Reduce liquid until almost dry (au sec).
4. Add 3 1/2 cups stock, sachet d'epices, and mushrooms. Cover sauce and bring to boil. Reduce heat and simmer uncovered 15 to 20 minutes. Skim occasionally. Discard sachet.
5. In small bowl, whisk remaining 1/2 cup stock to roux. Add to stock pot and simmer 10 to 15 minutes.
6. Strain sauce through chinois and return sauce to pot.
7. Add garlic paste mixture to pot to and simmer another 5 minutes or until nappé consistency is achieved. Adjust seasoning to taste.
