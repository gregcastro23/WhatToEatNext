# Mornay Sauce

**Yield:** 1 quart

## Ingredients

- 12 ounces onion (1 large), piqué
- 1 quart milk
- 1 ounce all-purpose flour (3 tablespoons)
- 1 ounce clarified butter
- Sea salt and white pepper to taste
- Pinch ground nutmeg
- 4 ounces gruyere cheese, grated (2 cups)

## Instructions

1. In 2 1/2 quart sauce pan, over low flame, add onion piqué to milk. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring often to avoid scorching.
2. Remove piqué onion from pot. Set milk aside to cool.
3. In 10-inch sauté pan, combine flour and butter. Stir with wooden spoon for 2-3 minutes. Let roux cool.
4. Slowly and gradually whisk cooled milk into warm roux.
5. Simmer sauce for approximately 20-30 minutes until thickened and reduced to nappé consistency.
6. Strain sauce through fine chinois.
7. Add cheese and whisk until cheese is totally melted and incorporated into sauce.
