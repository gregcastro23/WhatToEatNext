# APPLE-MUSTARD SAUCE

**Yield:** 6 cups

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 pound shallots (about 7 large), minced
- 8 ounces onion (1 medium), small dice
- 1/2 pound carrots (2 medium), 1/4-inch half-moon cut
- 1 pound apples (2 medium), peeled, cored, thinly sliced
- 1 cup apple juice
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 8 cups brown stock, warmed
- Sachet d'Epices (3 bay leaves, 6 parsley stems, 5 sprigs thyme, 6 black peppercorns tied in cheesecloth)
- 4 ounces blond roux (3 ounces flour + 2 ounces oil), cooled
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat oil over medium-high flame. Sauté shallots, onion, carrots, and apples until lightly caramelized.
2. Deglaze pan with apple juice and vinegar. Reduce liquid until almost dry (au sec). Do not remove vegetables.
3. Transfer vegetables to 1-gallon pot. Add mustard, 7 cups of warm brown stock, and sachet to pot. Bring to simmer.
4. In medium bowl, add 1 cup of remaining stock to roux. Whisk to combine.
5. Return stock and roux mixture to pot. Simmer 15 to 20 minutes until sauce thickens and nappé consistency is achieved.
6. Strain sauce through chinois. Adjust seasonings to taste.
