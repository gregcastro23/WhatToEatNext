# Apple Phyllo Roll

**Yield:** 2 phyllo rolls, sixteen 1 1/2-inch slices

## Ingredients

- 4 Fuji apples (2 pounds)
- Juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 4 tablespoons coconut sugar (buzzed to powder in coffee grinder)
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 8 sheets phyllo dough
- 1/4 cup canola oil
- 1/3 cup walnuts, oven-roasted and ground in food processor to breadcrumb texture
- Lemon and Maple-Flavored Yogurt (recipe below)

## Instructions

1. Preheat conventional oven to 400°F (convection oven will dry phyllo too much).
2. Prepare large bowl of water and add lemon juice. Peel, core, and thinly slice apples to 1/8-inch thickness and transfer them to lemon water to prevent browning.
3. Drain apples in colander. In 12-inch sauté pan, heat oil. Add apples, coconut sugar, and cinnamon. Sauté apples on medium heat until they release most of their moisture and become tender, stirring frequently. Transfer apples to bowl to cool.
4. Unroll phyllo on work surface and cover with damp cloth. To assemble, lay one sheet of phyllo on parchment paper horizontally. Brush sheet with oil and sprinkle lightly with small amount of walnuts. Repeat process, stacking and brushing another 3 sheets.
5. Spoon apple mixture horizontally along bottom of phyllo, leaving 1-inch border on bottom and 2 inches on left and right side. Fold in left and right sides over filling, then roll phyllo from bottom to top to form log. Repeat steps 5-7 for the second phyllo roll.
6. Score both rolls halfway through on bias into 8 equal portions. Brush roll with remaining oil.
7. Transfer rolls to parchment-lined sheet tray and bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden.
8. Cool ten minutes, then slice all the way through pre-scored cuts. Serve with Lemon and Maple-Flavored Yogurt (recipe below).
