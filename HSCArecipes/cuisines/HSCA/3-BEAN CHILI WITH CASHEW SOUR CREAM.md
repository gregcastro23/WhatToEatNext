# 3-BEAN CHILI WITH CASHEW SOUR CREAM

**Yield:** 8 cups

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 12 ounces onions (1 large), small dice
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 2 tablespoons chili powder
- 2 tablespoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1/4 cup minced garlic (4 large cloves)
- 6 ounces carrot (1 medium carrot), large dice
- 2 avocado leaves
- 2/3 cup pinto beans, soaked overnight, drained and rinsed
- 2/3 cup black beans, soaked overnight, drained and rinsed
- 2/3 cup kidney beans, soaked overnight, drained and rinsed
- 4 cups vegetable stock
- 14 ounce can whole tomatoes, chopped (reserve juice from can)
- 1/3 cup frozen corn or 1-2 ears fresh corn in season, kernels removed
- 2 limes, juiced
- 1 lime, cut into thin rounds
- 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro (1/2 ounce)
- 1/3 bunch scallions, thinly sliced
- Cashew Sour Cream (recipe below)

## Instructions

1. In tall, 2-gallon pressure cooker, heat oil over medium flame. Add onion, salt, chili powder, cumin, oregano, tomato paste, and garlic. When ingredients start to sizzle, immediately add carrots and avocado leaves. Sweat vegetables for 10-15 minutes, with pressure cooker lid slightly ajar, stirring occasionally.
2. Add pinto beans, black beans, kidney beans, and vegetable stock. Secure lid of pressure cooker, bring cooker to pressure, reduce heat, and cook for about 30 minutes.
3. Bring pressure down by running cold water on lid of cooker. Open pressure cooker and add tomatoes, reserved tomato juice, and corn. Simmer uncovered until chili thickens to desired consistency. Remove avocado leaves.
4. Add lime juice to taste. Garnish with lime rounds, cilantro, scallions, and Cashew Sour Cream.
