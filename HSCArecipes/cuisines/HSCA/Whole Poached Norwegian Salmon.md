# Whole Poached Norwegian Salmon

*Year-Round*

**Yield:** 25-30 servings (approximately 3 ounces each)

## Ingredients

- 2 quarts white wine
- 1 gallon water
- 4 lemons, sliced
- 3 bay leaves
- 2 tablespoons peppercorns
- 1 bunch dill
- 6 pounds salmon
- 2 cucumbers, thinly sliced on mandolin
- 2 bunches watercress, stemmed
- 6 lemons, thinly sliced
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 package radishes, slice
- 1 bunch parsley
- Salt to taste

## Instructions

1. Combine ingredients for court bouillon in deep hotel pan large enough to hold salmon. Bring to poach temperature.
2. Wrap salmon in double layer of cheesecloth leaving about 6 - 8 inches at each end of fish.
3. Twist and knot ends of cheesecloth. Cheesecloth is used to lift salmon out of pan when cooking is completed.
4. Lower salmon into court bouillon. For accurate cooking time, measure fish at its thickest point. Allow 10 minutes to the inch. At completion, internal temperature of fish should be 140°F.
5. Remove hotel pan from heat, and let fish cool in bouillon. Drain.
6. When totally cooled remove skin with paring knife. Decorate surface of fish with thinly sliced cucumbers.
7. Finish garnishing plate with watercress, lemon slices, cherry tomatoes, radishes, etc.
