# ROMESCO SAUCE

**Yield:** 2 cups

## Ingredients

- 1 pound plum tomatoes (3 large), concassé
- 10 ounces red peppers (2 medium)
- 1/3 ounce ancho chili (1 medium)
- 6 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic (1 large clove)
- 1/4 cup hazelnuts, toasted, skins rubbed off
- 1/4 cup blanched almonds, toasted
- 2 teaspoons sherry vinegar or to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt or to taste

## Instructions

1. Combine tomatoes in small bowl and set aside.
2. Arrange peppers on stove top, over direct flame, turning often as peppers become blackened and blistered. Transfer peppers to small bowl and cover. Allow peppers steam for 10 minutes. When peppers are cool, remove skins and seeds.
3. Heat cast iron skillet over medium flame. Toast chili each side until flexible. Do not scorch.
4. Remove seeds and transfer chili to 2 1/2 quart pot with water to cover. Simmer until chili softens, about 5 minutes. Drain chilis and set them aside, reserving soaking water.
5. In 10-inch sauté pan, heat 2 tablespoons oil over low flame, add garlic, and sweat until garlic has softened. Add tomatoes and peppers. Increase heat to medium-high and sauté until vegetables begin to caramelize.
6. In food processor, combine tomatoes, chili, hazelnuts, almonds, vinegar, and remaining 1/4 cup of oil. Blend until smooth. If sauce is too thick, add chili soaking water to processor to thin sauce.
